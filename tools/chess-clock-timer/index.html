Free Online Chess Clock

This free online chess clock lets two players manage their game time directly in the browser with no download or app required. It supports all major tournament time controls including Fischer increment, Bronstein delay, simple delay, and standard timing.

How to Use the Chess Clock

1. Set Up Your Time Control

Choose a preset time control (1+0, 3+2, 5+0, 10+5, etc.) or enter custom minutes and increment. Toggle different time per player if needed, select your timing method, and press Start.

2. During the Game

Tap your side of the clock after making a move to switch the timer to your opponent. On desktop, use keyboard keys or spacebar. Press Pause to pause both clocks and Reset to restart.

3. Fullscreen Mode

Use fullscreen mode for distraction-free over-the-board games on shared tablets. Press Esc to exit fullscreen.

Chess Time Controls Explained

What Is Fischer Increment?

Fischer increment adds a fixed number of seconds after every move. In a 5+3 game, each player starts with 5 minutes and receives 3 seconds per move.

What Is Bronstein Delay?

With Bronstein delay, time is restored only up to the amount used in the move. If you use 1 second on a 3-second delay, you only get 1 second back.
