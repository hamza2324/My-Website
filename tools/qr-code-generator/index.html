Error Correction Levels Explained

QR codes can still be scanned even when partially damaged or obscured, thanks to error correction. Choose the right level for your use case:

  • L (Low) — recovers up to 7% damage. Best for small, clean digital uses.
  • M (Medium) — recovers up to 15% damage. Recommended for most cases.
  • Q (Quartile) — recovers up to 25% damage. Good for printed materials.
  • H (High) — recovers up to 30% damage. Best when adding a logo or if the QR may be partially covered.

Are QR Codes Free to Use Commercially?

Yes. The QR code standard was published by Denso Wave as an open ISO standard (ISO/IEC 18004). While "QR Code" is a registered trademark of Denso Wave Inc., the technology itself is freely licensed. All QR codes generated on this page are free for personal and commercial use — print them, embed them, share them, without any restrictions from us.

Do QR Codes Expire?

Static QR codes — like the ones generated here — never expire. The data is encoded directly into the QR image itself. There is no server or redirect involved, so the code will continue to work as long as the destination (e.g. the website it links to) exists. Some paid services create "dynamic" QR codes with redirects that can expire if you stop paying — this tool does not do that.

QR Code Generator vs QR Code Scanner

A QR code generator creates QR codes from text, URLs, or data. A QR code scanner reads QR codes using a camera. This tool is a generator — if you need to scan a QR code online from an image or webcam, you can use a dedicated scanner tool.
